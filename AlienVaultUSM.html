Project Overview

This project focuses on implementing AlienVault USM to provide centralized threat detection and response capabilities, ensuring comprehensive protection of sensitive organizational daata while enhancing network visibility and compliance with security standards

Key Goals and Objectives

Comprehensive Threat Management:

  • Deploy AlienVault USM to centralize threat detection, log management and incident response.

Network Visibility and Security:

  • Enhance visibility across systems, identify vulnerabilities and ensure compliance with security standards.

Proactive Defense:

  • Protect sensitive data and create a secure environment for users.

Technologies Used

The deployment utilizes AlienVault USM software integrated with existing systems, supported by robust hardware infrastructure such as servers and networking devices, while adhering to industry-standard cybersecurity frameworks and certifications.

Software:

  • AlienVault USM, Discord, Microsoft Teams, Cognito ticketing system.

Hardware:

  • Servers, networking hardware, laptops/PCs.

Frameworks/Standards:

  • ISO 27001, NIST CSF, GDPR, AlienVault Certified Security Engineer (ACSE).

Additional Information

Stakeholders, vendors, and team members collaborate through secure communication platforms like Teams and Discord, ensuring seamless coordination. The project involves multi-phase implementation with rigorous testing, sandbox validation, and post-deployment support to guarantee a successful rollout by May 2025.

Stakeholders and Team:

  • Involves senior management, IT teams, and vendors like AT&T, CrowdStrike, and HackerOne.

Communication:

  • Regular updates through Teams, Discord, and emails; weekly progress reports.

Challenges:

  • Includes testing, integration, and ensuring low latency in alerts; a sandbox environment will validate functionality.

Timeline:

  • Multi-phase implementation with completion targeted for May 2025.
